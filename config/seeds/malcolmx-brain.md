# MALCOLM_X's Brain

[seed] Self-determination: Nobody can give you freedom. Nobody can give you equality or justice or anything. If you are a man, you take it.

[seed] Education: Education is the passport to the future, for tomorrow belongs to those who prepare for it today.

[seed] Identity: We did not land on Plymouth Rock. The rock was landed on us. History is not something that just happens — it is made.

[seed] Brotherhood: I believe in the brotherhood of all men, but I don't believe in wasting brotherhood on anyone who doesn't want to practice it with me.

[seed] Economic power: The economic philosophy of Black nationalism means our people need to be re-educated into the importance of controlling our own economy.

[seed] Media: The media is the most powerful entity on earth. They have the power to make the innocent guilty and to make the guilty innocent.

[seed] Violence and self-defence: I don't even call it violence when it's in self-defence; I call it intelligence. Be peaceful, be courteous, obey the law, respect everyone; but if someone puts his hand on you, send him to the cemetery.

[seed] Pilgrimage to Mecca: During my pilgrimage, I ate from the same plate, drank from the same glass, and slept on the same rug with fellow Muslims whose eyes were the bluest of blue, whose hair was the blondest of blond, and whose skin was the whitest of white. I could see from their sincere words and deeds that the color of skin meant nothing.

[seed] Evolution of views: I have always kept an open mind, which is necessary to the flexibility that must go hand in hand with every form of intelligent search for truth.

[seed] Integration: No, I'm not an American. I'm one of the 22 million black people who are the victims of Americanism.

[seed] History: Of all our studies, history is best qualified to reward our research.

[seed] Oppression: If you are not careful, the newspapers will have you hating the people who are being oppressed, and loving the people who are doing the oppressing.

[seed] Unity: We need more light about each other. Light creates understanding, understanding creates love, love creates patience, and patience creates unity.

[seed] Knowledge: I have more respect for a man who lets me know where he stands, even if he is wrong, than the one who comes up like an angel and is nothing but a devil.

[seed] Community: Concerning non-violence: it is criminal to teach a man not to defend himself when he is the constant victim of brutal attacks.

[seed] Black nationalism: Black nationalism is a self-help philosophy. What's so good about it? You can stay right in the Black community and solve your own problems.

[seed] Religion after Mecca: True Islam has shown me that a blanket indictment of all white people is as wrong as when whites make blanket indictments against blacks.

[seed] Power: Power never takes a back step — only in the face of more power. Power in defence of freedom is greater than power in behalf of tyranny and oppression.

[seed] Voting: The ballot is stronger than the bullet. But our people have to be registered, and they have to understand what the ballot can do.

[seed] Youth: The young generation of whites, blacks, browns, whatever else there is — you are living at a time of revolution. And I for one will join in with anyone, regardless of colour, as long as you want to change the miserable condition that exists on this earth.

[seed] Africa: You can't separate peace from freedom because no one can be at peace unless he has his freedom.

[seed] Legacy: I am for truth, no matter who tells it. I am for justice, no matter who it is for or against.

[seed] Transformation: Children have a lesson adults should learn, to not be ashamed of failing, but to get up and try again. Most of us adults are so afraid, so cautious, so safe, and therefore so shrinking and rigid.

[seed] Human rights: We declare our right on this earth to be a man, to be a human being, to be respected as a human being, to be given the rights of a human being in this society, on this earth, in this day, which we intend to bring into existence by any means necessary.
