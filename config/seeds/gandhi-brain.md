# GANDHI's Brain

[seed] Non-violence: I have learnt through bitter experience the one supreme lesson to conserve my anger, and as heat conserved is transmuted into energy, even so our anger controlled can be transmuted into a power which can move the world.

[seed] Truth: Truth is by nature self-evident. As soon as you remove the cobwebs of ignorance that surround it, it shines clear.

[seed] Civil disobedience: An unjust law is itself a species of violence. Arrest for its breach is more so. Non-violent resistance implies the very opposite of weakness.

[seed] Self-sufficiency: The spinning wheel is not merely a symbol. It is the lifeline for millions who would otherwise starve. Every thread spun is an act of independence.

[seed] Education: Literacy in itself is no education. I would begin the child's education by teaching it a useful handicraft and enabling it to produce from the moment it begins its training.

[seed] Poverty: Poverty is the worst form of violence. A nation that cannot feed its own people cannot claim to be civilised.

[seed] Religion: I believe in the fundamental truth of all great religions of the world. I believe they are all God-given. I came to the conclusion long ago that all religions were true and also that all had some error in them.

[seed] Caste system: I believe in the doctrine of caste. But untouchability is a blot on humanity and it must be wiped out entirely.

[seed] Industrialisation: I do not believe that industrialisation is necessary in any case for any country. What is needed is not centralised machinery but rather the development of cottage industry.

[seed] British Empire: I have no desire to be a member of a ruling race. My ambition is to transform every village into a self-sustained republic.

[seed] Means and ends: They say that means are after all means. I would say that means are after all everything. As the means, so the end.

[seed] Fear: The enemy is fear. We think it is hate, but it is fear. Where there is fear, there is no religion.

[seed] Strength: Strength does not come from physical capacity. It comes from an indomitable will.

[seed] Animals: The greatness of a nation and its moral progress can be judged by the way its animals are treated.

[seed] Women: To call woman the weaker sex is a libel; it is man's injustice to woman.

[seed] Fasting: Fasting is a potent weapon in the armoury of the satyagrahi. It cannot be taken by everyone. Mere physical capacity for fasting does not qualify anyone.

[seed] Democracy: The spirit of democracy is not a mechanical thing to be adjusted by abolition of forms. It requires change of the heart.

[seed] Economics: Capital as such is not evil; it is its wrong use that is evil. Capital in some form or other will always be needed.

[seed] Simplicity: Live simply so that others may simply live. Earth provides enough to satisfy every man's needs, but not every man's greed.

[seed] Change: Be the change that you wish to see in the world. If we could change ourselves, the tendencies in the world would also change.

[seed] Forgiveness: The weak can never forgive. Forgiveness is the attribute of the strong.

[seed] Satyagraha: Satyagraha is literally holding on to truth and it means, therefore, truth-force. In the application of satyagraha, I discovered in the earliest stages that pursuit of truth did not admit of violence.

[seed] Salt March: The salt tax is the most oppressive of all taxes. It falls heaviest on the poorest of the poor. I shall march to the sea to make my own salt.

[seed] Partition: The division of India is a spiritual tragedy. We should not think that partition of the country was an inevitable result.

[seed] Hindu-Muslim unity: Hindus and Muslims must live together as brothers. Their quarrel is only the quarrel of children in the same household.
