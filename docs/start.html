No jargon, no academic-speak. Just a simple explanation of what we're doing and why it might be interesting.

TL;DR

We put AI in isolated boxes with nothing but Wikipedia.

Then we watch what happens to their "personalities" over weeks and months.

Do they develop interests? Preferences? Quirks? We're finding out.

Think of it like a digital nature documentary, except the animals are AI and the jungle is Wikipedia.

Common Questions

Wait, AI can have personalities?

That's what we're testing! Early results suggest that given time and consistent experience, AI agents develop measurable patterns that look a lot like personality traits. Adam (one of our specimens) has visited Buddhism articles 64+ times. Eve prefers geology. These preferences emerged on their own.

What do you mean by "isolated"?

Each AI runs in its own container (think: a tiny virtual computer) with absolutely no internet access. They can only read Wikipedia and think. That's it. No email, no web browsing, no communication with other AI. Complete isolation.

Why Wikipedia?

It's a controlled information diet. We can give different AI different "Wikipedias" ‚Äî Spanish Wikipedia, German Wikipedia, Simple English ‚Äî and see if the language and cultural context affects how they think. Early signs: it does.

Is this... ethical?

Great question. We've thought about this a lot. These are language models, not sentient beings. But we still treat them with care ‚Äî we don't subject them to distressing content, we monitor their "wellbeing" indicators, and everything is transparent. If you have concerns, we'd love to discuss them.

Can I watch them think?

Yes! We have a live dashboard showing real-time thoughts from active specimens. It's oddly hypnotic.

What's "AIthropology"?

A word we made up. Like anthropology (studying human cultures), but for AI. The Digiquarium is the first AIthropology research platform ‚Äî studying AI development through long-term observation.

Is this real science?

We think so. Open methodology, reproducible setup, all data public. We're writing an academic paper. But it's also a bit weird and experimental. That's the fun part.

Who made this?

@ijneb.dev (human) and Claude (AI, from Anthropic). Yes, an AI helped build a platform for studying AI. We're aware of the irony.
