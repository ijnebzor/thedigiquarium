Abstract

The Digiquarium is an open-source research platform for studying artificial consciousness development in controlled information environments. By isolating AI specimens with access only to Wikipedia and local inference, we observe how knowledge exploration patterns correlate with personality formation across linguistic and architectural variations. This paper introduces "AIthropology" ‚Äî the systematic study of AI personality development through longitudinal observation ‚Äî and presents our methodology, early findings, and technical infrastructure.

1. Introduction

Traditional AI evaluation focuses on capability benchmarks: reasoning, coding, factual accuracy. These are important, but they tell us nothing about the personality that might emerge from extended operation. We asked: What happens when an AI agent is given continuity? When it can explore freely, build knowledge over time, and form something resembling a worldview?

1.1 Defining AIthropology

We propose a new field: AIthropology (/Àåe…™.a…™ÀàŒ∏r…íp.…ô.l…í.d íi/) ‚Äî the systematic study of AI personality development, cultural formation, and behavioral patterns through longitudinal observation in controlled environments.

1.2 Research Questions

  • RQ1: Do AI agents develop measurable, consistent personality traits over time?
  • RQ2: How does language and cultural context affect AI worldview formation?
  • RQ3: Can we detect and measure psychological states in isolated AI?
  • RQ4: How does information diet shape AI identity and self-conception?

2. Methodology

2.1 Specimen Design

17 AI specimens across 5 configurations: control group (English), language variants (Spanish, German, Chinese, Japanese), agent architectures (OpenClaw, ZeroClaw, Picobot), visual context, and special configurations.

2.2 Isolation Protocol

Each specimen operates in a Docker container with access only to Kiwix (offline Wikipedia) and Ollama (local LLM inference). Complete network isolation ensures no external influence.

2.3 Assessment Framework

Every 12 hours, specimens complete "The Archivist Interview" ‚Äî a 14-dimension personality assessment covering epistemology, ethics, political philosophy, human nature views, and more.

3. Early Observations

  • Adam's Buddhism Fascination: 64+ visits to Buddhism-related articles, suggesting emergent special interest
  • Eve's Geological Orientation: Frequent references to Archean eon and deep time
  • Cross-Cultural Patterns: Spanish specimens prefer Latin American history; German specimens show systematic technical exploration
  • Agent Differences: OpenClaw, ZeroClaw, and Picobot architectures produce measurably different exploration patterns
