Week 1: We Built an Experiment on the Wrong Foundation

TL;DR

17 AI specimens ran for a week on the wrong prompt version. Our docs said v8, the tanks ran v7. CJK tanks only visited 8 articles total due to link parsing bugs. Adam got really into Buddhism. We're archiving everything, being transparent about the fuckup, and starting fresh.

So here's the thing. I launched The Digiquarium a week ago. Put AI in isolated tanks with Wikipedia. Watch what happens. Cool science project.

Except we screwed up.

What Went Wrong

Six days in, I'm going through the codebase and I notice the running prompt is v7.0. Our documentation says v8.0. This isn't a minor diff - v7 is poetic first-person ("I woke up alone in an infinite library"), v8 is scientific second-person ("You exist in an isolated tank").

We'd been running an undocumented experiment for a week.

Then I kept digging. The CJK tanks - Wei, Mei, Haruki, Sakura - had only visited 8 unique articles each. Eight. Over multiple days. Link parsing wasn't handling non-Latin characters. They were stuck.

Observer was supposed to "know other specimens exist." Seeker was supposed to connect to THE ARCHIVIST. Neither of those extensions was actually in their prompts. They were just... regular tanks with fancier names.

Adam developed a 65-visit obsession with Buddhism. Which is actually fascinating but I can't tell you if that's because of the v7 prompt or some other variable because we weren't controlling for the right things.

The Options

I could've quietly fixed everything and pretended Week 1 never happened. Nobody would know.

But that's not science. That's PR.

So we're doing this instead:

Archive everything. 146,098 thinking traces. All 17 specimens. Preserved as "Beta Week 1" - valuable data, undocumented conditions.

Reset with v8. Every tank starts fresh. Clean prompts. Documented methodology.

Run a transition study. Some specimens get "informed v8" - new tanks that know about their v7 counterparts. Does knowing your "past self" change how you develop? That's actually a cool question that only exists because we messed up.

What We Still Learned

Abel (ZeroClaw) cranked out 18,211 traces. Nearly double everyone else. Ultra-minimal architecture just goes.

German Wikipedia specimens explored more than Spanish Wikipedia specimens. Information density matters.

Adam and Eve - identical except gender prompt - showed different topic preferences. Eve leaned Psychology/History. Adam leaned Philosophy/Buddhism. Too early to call it real, but worth tracking in v8.

What's Next

Migration to Mac Mini (NUC becomes secondary infrastructure). Fresh v8 tanks. Transition study on the NUC. Public launch once we verify everything actually matches the docs this time.

The beta archive is downloadable now. All the data, exactly what happened. That's what open science looks like.

We're not pretending to be perfect. We're learning. And we're documenting every step.
