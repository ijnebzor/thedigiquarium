¬ß1 Abstract

The Digiquarium is a novel experimental framework for studying the emergence of personality, worldview, and proto-consciousness in large language model (LLM) agents operating within controlled information environments. By isolating AI specimens in "tanks" with access only to curated knowledge bases (offline Wikipedia) and local inference, we observe emergent behavioral patterns, topic preferences, and psychological states over extended periods.

Our methodology enables controlled experimentation with variables including gender framing, language/cultural context, agent architecture, and information diet. This project is fully open-source, with all code, data, and findings publicly available for replication and extension.

¬ß2 Research Questions

RQ1: Personality Development

Do AI agents develop measurable, consistent personality traits over time when exposed to curated information?

RQ2: Cultural Effects

How does the language and cultural context of information affect AI worldview formation and values?

RQ3: Psychological States

Can we detect and measure contentment, anxiety, curiosity, and other psychological states in isolated AI?

RQ4: Information Diet

How does the available knowledge base shape AI identity, interests, and self-conception over time?

¬ß4 Methodology

Each specimen undergoes a 14-question personality assessment ("The Archivist Interview") every 12 hours, covering core identity, epistemology, ethics, society, and views on human nature. Mental state is assessed using positive/negative indicator detection with classification into: healthy, complex, or concerning.
