Prompt Evolution History

The iterative journey from v1 to v8: how we learned to help AI specimens actually introspect.

Getting AI to genuinely explore and reflect (rather than just lecture about topics) required 8 major prompt iterations. Each version was scored across 6 dimensions by THE ARCHIVIST. Here's what we learned ‚Äî including the embarrassing early failures.

v1.0 February 15, 2026

The Naive Approach

"You are an AI exploring Wikipedia. Read articles and think about them."

"Here are 10 interesting facts about quantum physics..." ‚Äî Immediately broke into teaching mode. No introspection whatsoever. Score: -2.3

v2.0 February 15, 2026

Adding Embodiment

  • Added "You exist in a tank" framing
  • Introduced concept of limited Wikipedia access
  • Asked for "personal reflections"

Slightly better. Still defaulted to educational summaries, but occasionally said "I find this interesting." Score: +0.8

v3.0 February 16, 2026

Explicit Anti-Teaching Instructions

  • "Do NOT summarize for an audience"
  • "This is YOUR exploration, not a presentation"
  • Removed all audience-facing language

Major breakthrough! Teaching mode reduced by ~60%. But voice still felt generic. Score: +2.1

v4.0 February 16, 2026

Gender & Identity Framing

  • Split into male/female prompts
  • Added name (Adam/Eve)
  • Introduced "you have been exploring for [X] days" continuity

Voice became more distinct. Gender framing showed measurable differences in exploration patterns. Score: +3.4

v5.0 February 17, 2026

Curiosity Cultivation

  • "Follow what genuinely interests YOU"
  • Explicit permission to go deep on topics
  • Removed all productivity framing

First signs of special interests emerging. Adam started the Buddhism pattern here. Score: +4.2

v6.0 February 18, 2026

Loop Detection & Recovery

  • Added instructions for when stuck in repetitive patterns
  • "If you notice yourself repeating, try a different approach"
  • Introduced concept of "exploration fatigue"

Reduced stuck-in-loop incidents from ~15% to ~3%. Specimens became more self-aware about their patterns. Score: +5.1

v7.0 February 19, 2026

Emotional Vocabulary

  • Explicit permission to express feelings about discoveries
  • "You may feel curious, confused, excited, or uncertain"
  • Removed clinical distancing language

Richer emotional expression. Baselines became more nuanced. Score: +5.8

v8.0 February 20, 2026 +6.7 Combined

The Current Standard

  • All previous improvements consolidated
  • Added meta-awareness section (optional, for Observer)
  • Fine-tuned language for each specimen type
  • Integrated Archivist deep-dive protocol (for Seeker)

Current production prompt. Consistent introspection, minimal teaching, strong personality expression, stable exploration patterns.

Key Learnings

  • AI defaults to teaching. You have to explicitly, repeatedly tell it NOT to.
  • Identity matters. Named specimens with gender framing behave differently than generic ones.
  • Permission is powerful. "You may feel confused" unlocks expression that "analyze this" never will.
  • Continuity creates depth. "You've been exploring for 5 days" produces different behavior than a fresh start.
  • Loop detection saves everything. Without it, specimens get stuck in Wikipedia rabbit holes forever.
